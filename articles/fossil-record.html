ORGANIZED COMPLEXITY DOES NOT COME ABOUT BY ACCIDENT

By Wealthy Emeseh • December 12, 2025 • 3 min read

Picture a wall of light bulbs. At first they simply blink, nothing more than flashes of light. But suddenly the pattern changes. Dot dot dot, dash dash dash, dot dot dot. It is Morse code for SOS. That is not chaos. That is communication. That is functional, organized complexity carrying meaning and purpose. No one sees that pattern and shrugs, thinking it just happened. The moment the randomness becomes a message, you know a mind is behind it. Now imagine those same lights flickering with no rhythm or structure. Nothing but noise. No message. No purpose. Just random flashes going nowhere. You would never call that communication. You would never assume intelligence. You would instantly know it was meaningless and undirected. That is the difference between randomness and design. Inside every cell of your body is something far more astonishing than blinking lights. A ribosome. A microscopic biomolecular machine that reads genetic instructions and assembles proteins with precision. It follows a code. It obeys instructions. It builds structures, enzymes, tissues, and ultimately you. This machine does not spit out random junk. It produces exactly what the coded information tells it to produce, one protein after another with intention, order, and purpose. This is why Dr. James Tour, one of the most accomplished synthetic chemists on earth, asks a direct question the scientific world cannot answer: How did this begin without a mind? He has spent decades working on molecular machines, and his verdict is blunt. Abiogenesis is not just unlikely. It is chemically impossible. You cannot get a functional ribosome by chance. You cannot get the coded instructions by chance. You cannot even get the basic building blocks assembled in the right way. The chemistry fights against it at every step. Order and instruction do not rise out of chaos. They come from intelligence. Life is not just complexity. It is meaningful, functional, specific, code-driven complexity. Like blinking lights that deliver a message. Like machinery that follows a blueprint. Like a Designer whose fingerprints cover everything He made. A code requires a code writer. Design demands a Designer.
